Twin Songs

This page will compare the sound and themes behind pairs of songs with the same names.

Adios

  • Everglow

    This K-Pop group released their song named "Adios" in 2019. Its music video exemplifies the common K-Pop concept, "girl crush," with the choreography as its main focus. Though its genre is debated upon, it's a powerful EDM song in a style common among K-Pop groups. Its lyrics harshly tell someone to, essentially, get lost.

  • Hoody, Gray

    This KR&B song was also released in 2019. The music video takes place in a convenience store and includes a fight scene. This song is a staple KR&B song that any fan of the genre should know. Its lyrics are about escaping the restrictions of everyday life and feeling free and happy.

Airplane Mode

  • Fromis_9

    This K-Pop group released their song named "Airplane Mode" in 2021 as a side track. It's a very poppy K-Pop dance song with a nice piano line, solid beat drop, and catchy chorus. It's fun, motivational, and relaxing to listen to all at once. Its lyrics are about the excitement of traveling the world with someone you love. Some of the lyrics suggest that the world travel is metaphorical and actually represents surfing the internet.

  • Limbo

    This is a dreamy pop song that gained popularity through a YouTube upload from 2017, though its official release date is in 2018. Its lyrics describe a girl's feelings in a complicated relationship, and it seems that a lot of the complication is coming from the relationship being a long-distance one.

Colors

  • Halsey

    This electropop song was released in 2015 as part of one of the artist's most popular albums to date, "Badlands". This song and album played a huge part in a generation's pre-teen and teen years. The lyrics of this song are about some of the complex feelings one can have in a relationship. Despite its name being in plural, the song only focuses on the single color of blue.

  • Loona

    This K-Pop girl group song was released in 2019 as a side track. It has no official music video, but it does have choreography. This song is considered a fan-favorite. Its genre is electropop as well as alternative R&B. Its lyrics are about falling in love. Unlike its aforementioned name twin, it does include multiple colors as subjects of its lyrics.

Lie

  • EXID

    This K-Pop girl group released their song, stylized as "L.I.E," in 2016. It is a dance K-Pop song. Its music video, like many other K-Pop music videos at the time, is brightly-colored with a variety of symbolic scenes and a vague storyline. The lyrics berate a male love interest for talking to too many girls and being sort of a player.

  • Mad Clown, Lee Hae Ri

    This Korean rap song was released in 2017. Its sound can be described as chill, melancholic, and sad. Sandwiched between the rap verses is a ballad refrain. The song's music video depicts some of the good and bad moments of a couple's relationship with gorgeous cinematography and scenery. The lyrics are about a relationship fading and falling apart and the feelings of loss and confliction that come with it.

Love

  • f(x)

    This K-Pop song was released in 2011 as a side track on the group's first album, "Pinocchio". The group is known for experimental electropop, and that can be observed in this cute love song. Its sound is cutesy and bright. Its lyrics are about a love so powerful that it brightens up the darkest days.

  • Kendrick Lamar, Zacari

    This song was released in 2017 and is one of the artist's most popular and critically-acclaimed works to date. Its genre is hip hop and rap. Its music video features high-quality cinematography and gorgeous visuals with scenes depicting the good and bad moments of a relationship. Its sound can be described as relaxing. Its lyrics are about endless dedication to someone you love.

Lucid Dream

  • Aespa

    This K-Pop girl group song was released in 2021 as a side track on their popular EP, "Savage". Living up to its name, its genre is mainly dream pop. Like many K-Pop songs across multiple eras, it has a lot of R&B elements. It has no music video but does have an official choreography. Its lyrics poetically describe the feeling of falling in love.

  • Golden Child

    This K-Pop boy group song was released in 2020 as the title track of their fourth mini album, "Take a Leap". It's an EDM song with strong bass and synths. Its music video is full of fantastical, barren landscapes with glamor shots and choreography scenes. Its lyrics also poetically describe the feeling of falling in love.

Neon

  • DPR Live

    This K-Hip Hop/Rap song was released in 2020. It doesn't have its own video, and rather is blended with the artist's other song "Kiss Me" in a joint music video. The music video is themed after space exploration. The song's sound is very upbeat and danceable with heavy piano lines. Its lyrics are about being completely infatuated with someone.

  • Yukika

    This K-Pop song was released by the Japanese-Korean singer in 2019. Its genre is city pop and its sound is light and nostalgia-filled. Its music video follows the nostalgic city pop theme. Like its aforementioned name twin, the lyrics focus on neon lights. In this song, however, the lyrics use the neon lights as the backdrop for the singer's feelings rather than as the central metaphor. In 2020, Yukika released an alternative version of the song, "Neon 1989," which leans more heavily into the nostalgic city pop sound.

Nobody

  • Blue.D, Mino

    This KR&B song was released in 2019. The key feature of its music video is the various scenes of the singer being in many different rooms that seem to connect to each other through a portal-like window. The song's lyrics are about having a crush on someone and wishing that they would like you the same way. The refrain is characterized by the repetition of the word "Nobody" as the singer tells her love interest that she has eyes for nobody else.

  • Mitski

    This alternative indie song was released in 2018. Its music video shows the singer going through many strange phenomena. The lyrics are about an existential sense of loneliness. Its refrain also features the repetition of the word "nobody." This repetition has actually been widely used as a meme across many social media platforms.

Secret

  • Gain

    This relaxing K-Pop side track was released in 2016. It has no music video but does have choreography. It has a light, springtime sound. Its lyrics describe what it's like to have hidden feelings for someone.

  • WJSN

    This cutesy, dreamy K-Pop girl group song was also released in 2016. Its fantastical music video takes place in space. The members of the girl group roam a pink and orange planet, drive a space car, read floating books in a floating house, and much more. The lyrics of this song also describe what it's like to have hidden feelings for someone.

Secret Garden

  • IU

    This K-Pop song is actually a remake of a 2003 song by Lee Sang Eun, but upon its release in 2017 has completely eclipsed the original. It has no lyrics or choreography. Its sound is light, airy, and springtimey. The lyrics are about how everything lights up when you fall in love.

  • Oh My Girl

    This dreamy K-Pop girl group song was released in 2018. Its fantastical music video shows the group's members in outer space, on flying trains, and in magical underwater rooms. Its sound is relaxing and dreamy. Its lyrics describe what it's like to invite someone else into your inner world and imagination.

Siren

  • Sunmi

    This K-Pop song was released in 2018. Its music video begins with the singer sneezing in the mirror, something believed to cause a curse, before her alter-ego appears to torment her. The song's genre can be described as ambient pop. In the lyrics, the singer tells a boy to get away from her because she's sure she will hurt him.

  • Taeyeon

    This K-Pop song was released in 2022 as a side track on the artist's critically acclaimed album, "INVU". The song's instrumental features the widely-recognizable emergency siren. With soulful vocals, the singer describes what it can be like to witness someone fall in love with you.
